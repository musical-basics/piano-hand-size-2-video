## VO 01 - Late Night Drive

```text
The funny thing about this trip is that it only makes sense if you already care way too much about piano keys.

It was almost two in the morning, and I was driving to Pennsylvania because one person in the country had built the exact keyboards I wanted to try: DS 6.0 and DS 5.5.

On paper, that sounds ridiculous. But if you have smaller hands, a few millimeters on each key can change the whole experience of playing.
```

## VO 02 - Gas Station And Snacks

```text
So this became one of those very specific road trips where the goal is extremely serious, but the process is just gas stations, snacks, and questionable decisions.

I was trying not to drink caffeine, which sounds mature until you realize the backup plan was chocolate milk and sleeping in the car.
```

## VO 03 - Car Nap

```text
At some point, my body made the executive decision that the trip was over for a while.

I passed out in the back of the car, woke up a few hours later, and somehow felt like a human being again.

Not a fully restored human being. But enough to keep driving.
```

## VO 04 - Pennsylvania Road

```text
By morning, the trip started to feel real.

The highways got quieter, the roads got greener, and every mile made the whole thing feel a little less like an impulsive idea and more like a pilgrimage.

I had talked to David, I knew I was getting close, and I kept thinking: if these keyboards actually feel different, this whole drive is going to be worth it.
```

## VO 05 - Lake Pause

```text
After visiting David, I stopped by the lake for a minute just to breathe.

At that point I had the keyboards. The hard part was supposed to be over.

Which, of course, is exactly when the trip decided it needed one more plot twist.
```

## VO 06 - Breakdown And Return

```text
The car broke down in Pennsylvania, so the road trip turned into a much longer story than planned.

But honestly, that made the whole thing feel even more appropriate. This was never just about buying equipment.

It was about chasing the question that started the whole project: what happens when the piano finally fits the person playing it?
```
