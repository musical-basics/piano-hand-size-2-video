# YouTube Video Plan: Piano Hand Size Part 2

## Core Idea

This video should feel like a personal road quest with a real argument underneath it:

> I drove through the night to pick up rare DS 6.0 and DS 5.5 piano keyboards because hand size changes the entire way you experience the piano.

The strongest version is not a pure gear review and not a pure vlog. It is a story-driven explainer: the trip gives the video movement, and the keyboard/hand-size thesis gives it meaning.

Recommended length: 10 to 13 minutes.

## Best Title Directions

1. I Drove 11 Hours for a Smaller Piano Keyboard
2. Why Piano Hand Size Changes Everything
3. I Bought a DS 5.5 Keyboard. Here Is Why It Matters
4. The Piano Key Size Problem Most Pianists Ignore
5. Smaller Piano Keys Changed My Technique

Best thumbnail idea:

- Split image: night road/car on one side, close-up of DS keyboard keys on the other.
- Text: `SMALLER KEYS?` or `11 HOURS FOR THIS`
- Add a hand stretched over keys if you have a clean still.

## Narrative Spine

1. Hook: the commitment is absurd and compelling.
2. Problem: standard piano keys do not fit everyone equally.
3. Journey: overnight drive, gas stations, fatigue, Pennsylvania scenery.
4. Discovery: David, the DS keyboards, the different key sizes.
5. Thesis: alternate key sizes can change technique, comfort, and practice efficiency.
6. Complication: the car breaks down, which makes the trip feel like an actual saga.
7. Payoff: back home with DS 6.0 and DS 5.5, updated thoughts, demo/comparison.

Suggested on-screen chart:

`My current take`

| Handspan / situation | Recommendation |
| --- | --- |
| Smaller hands generally | Try DS 6.0 and DS 5.5 before assuming standard is the only option. |
| Under around 8.2 inches | DS 5.5 may be worth considering. |
| Around 7.2-7.5 inches | Preference can vary, so test both DS 6.0 and DS 5.5 if possible. |
| University / serious study context | Matching DS standard sizes matters. |

Make the chart quick and non-dogmatic: "try both" is the safest useful message.

## Edit Style

- Keep A-roll jumpy and tight.
- Use captions for key claims, not every word.
- Use map/time/location cards sparingly:
  - `1:42 a.m.`
  - `Baltimore area -> Titusville, PA`
  - `5.5 hours each way`
  - `DS 6.0 / DS 5.5`
- Use still photos as punch-ins during technical explanations.
- Avoid long uninterrupted explanations. Every 20-30 seconds, cut to hands, keys, road, factory, or a still.

## Optional Pickups That Would Help

If you can record two short clips, the video gets much cleaner:

1. A 20-second front-facing intro:

   > This is part two of my piano hand-size journey. In this video I drove to Pennsylvania to pick up DS 6.0 and DS 5.5 keyboards, and I want to show why these sizes matter for pianists with smaller hands.

2. A close-up hand/key comparison:

   - Your hand on standard keys.
   - Your hand on DS 6.0.
   - Your hand on DS 5.5.
   - Same chord or interval on each.

If you do not want to film more, use text cards and the `IMG_0310` home explanation instead.

## Best Final Message

The video should leave viewers with this:

> Standard piano keys are not neutral. For some pianists, key size affects technique, tension, repertoire, and confidence. DS 6.0 and DS 5.5 are not gimmicks. They are serious options that more pianists should be able to try.
